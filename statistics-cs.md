| Mean                  | The average value of a dataset, calculated by summing all values and dividing by the count.                    | Calculate the average monthly sales of a product over the past year.                                            |
| Median                | The middle value of a dataset when arranged in ascending order.                                                 | Determine the median household income in a city.                                                                |
| Mode                  | The most frequent value(s) in a dataset.                                                                        | Identify the most common type of car sold by a dealership.                                                      |
| Variance              | The measure of the dispersion of values in a dataset from the mean.                                             | Analyze the variance in daily stock prices over a month to assess market volatility.                            |
| Standard Deviation    | The square root of the variance, indicating the spread of data around the mean.                                 | Determine the standard deviation of test scores to understand student performance.                              |
| Correlation           | A measure describing the relationship between two variables.                                                    | Investigate the correlation between study time and exam scores.                                                 |
| Regression            | A statistical method to model the relationship between a dependent variable and one or more independent variables.| Use regression analysis to predict housing prices based on factors like location and size.                      |
| Hypothesis Testing    | A statistical method to test if there is a significant difference between two groups or conditions.             | Conduct hypothesis testing to determine if a new drug treatment is effective compared to a placebo.              |
| Confidence Interval   | A range of values in which the true population parameter is likely to lie with a certain level of confidence.   | Calculate a confidence interval for the average height of students in a school based on a sample of measurements.|
| Probability           | The measure of the likelihood that an event will occur.                                                         | Determine the probability of a customer clicking on an online ad based on past click-through rates.             |
| Outlier               | An observation that lies an abnormal distance from other values in a dataset.                                    | Identify outliers in weather data to investigate potential measurement errors.                                    |
| Quartile              | Values that divide a dataset into four equal parts, each containing 25% of the data.                             | Analyze income quartiles to understand income distribution in a population.                                      |
| Skewness              | A measure of the asymmetry of the probability distribution of a real-valued random variable.                     | Assess the skewness of stock returns to understand the risk associated with an investment portfolio.             |
| Kurtosis              | A measure of the "tailedness" of the probability distribution of a real-valued random variable.                  | Evaluate the kurtosis of a distribution to assess the likelihood of extreme events in financial markets.        |
| Normal Distribution   | A symmetric, bell-shaped probability distribution.                                                               | Model test scores as normally distributed to analyze student performance.                                        |
| Binomial Distribution | A discrete probability distribution of the number of successes in a fixed number of independent Bernoulli trials.| Use the binomial distribution to model the number of successful sales calls in a day.                              |
| Poisson Distribution  | A discrete probability distribution that expresses the probability of a given number of events occurring in a fixed interval of time or space. | Model the number of customer arrivals per hour in a store.                                                     |
| Chi-Square Test       | A statistical test used to determine if there is a significant association between two categorical variables.    | Conduct a chi-square test to evaluate the relationship between smoking habits and lung cancer incidence.          |
| ANOVA                 | Analysis of Variance, a statistical method used to compare means of three or more samples.                       | Use ANOVA to determine if there is a significant difference in test scores among students from different schools.|
| T-Test                | A statistical test used to determine if there is a significant difference between the means of two groups.     | Conduct a t-test to compare the effectiveness of two teaching methods on student performance.                    |
| F-Test                | A statistical test used to compare the variances of two populations.                                             | Use an F-test to assess if there is a significant difference in the variance of IQ scores between two age groups. |
| Central Limit Theorem | A fundamental theorem in probability theory that states that the sampling distribution of the sample means approaches a normal distribution as the sample size increases. | Apply the central limit theorem to estimate the average weight of apples in a shipment based on a sample.      |
| Confidence Level      | The probability that a parameter falls within a specified range of values.                                       | Calculate a 95% confidence level for the proportion of voters who support a political candidate.                   |
| Degrees of Freedom    | The number of values in the final calculation of a statistic that are free to vary.                               | Determine the degrees of freedom in a t-test based on the sample size and the number of groups being compared.    |
| P-Value               | The probability of obtaining results as extreme as the observed results of a statistical hypothesis test, assuming that the null hypothesis is true. | Interpret the p-value obtained from a hypothesis test to assess the strength of evidence against the null hypothesis. |
| Pearson Correlation Coefficient | A measure of the linear relationship between two continuous variables.                                        | Calculate the Pearson correlation coefficient between rainfall and crop yield to assess the impact of weather on agriculture. |
| Spearman's Rank Correlation | A non-parametric measure of the strength and direction of association between two ranked variables.             | Use Spearman's rank correlation to assess the relationship between the rankings of products by different consumers. |
| R-Squared             | A statistical measure that represents the proportion of the variance for a dependent variable that's explained by an independent variable. | Assess the goodness of fit of a regression model by examining the R-squared value.                                |
| Z-Score               | A standardized score that indicates how many standard deviations a data point is from the mean.                   | Calculate the z-score of a student's test score to compare their performance relative to their peers.            |
| Margin of Error       | The range within which the true population parameter is estimated to lie with a specified level of confidence.   | Determine the margin of error for a political opinion poll to assess the accuracy of the estimated results.      |
| Residuals             | The differences between observed and predicted values in a regression analysis.                                  | Analyze the residuals of a regression model to assess the model's accuracy and identify any patterns or trends.  |
| Out-of-Sample Testing | Testing a model's performance on data it hasn't seen before to evaluate its generalization ability.             | Conduct out-of-sample testing to assess the predictive accuracy of a machine learning algorithm.                 |
| Interquartile Range   | The range between the first and third quartiles, representing the middle 50% of the dataset.                    | Calculate the interquartile range of test scores to assess the spread of student performance.                   |
| Type I Error          | Incorrectly rejecting a true null hypothesis, also known as a false positive.                                    | Consider the consequences of a Type I error when conducting a hypothesis test, such as falsely accusing someone of a crime. |
